Happy Holidays

This is a mission on my part to throw my money into the anime industry. So accept the Crunchyroll membership, get ready, set, go watch. I've even compiled some recommendations for you


Fate/Zero

Are you a fan of mythology of any sort? Of shows where no character is left unscathed by the end of a show? Of existential crises and stunning animation (3D and 2D)? Watched Fate/Zero. Even if you're not a fan of any of the above, watch it anyway

Psychopass

A dystopian show in which the "Sibyl System" can arrest criminals before they commit the crime. If you watch, only watch the first season. The second is something of an abomination

Parasyte: the Maxim

A parasite/alien like creature crawls into the main character's ears and ends up sharing a body with him. Originally a fight for control over the body, their relationship becomes a fascinating codependence. The animation can be a bit unnerving, but once you get over that, it's a really deep show about what defines humanity (and the lack thereof).

May your holiday be full of relaxation, food, and anime.
Shelter

Only a 6 minute music video. So watch this (if you haven't already)! That's all I'm saying so just take my word for it. It's on youtube too.

Puella Magi Madoka Magica

This show is a mind f*** if I ever saw one. Again, I don't want to say too much as I'm a firm believer in spoiler-free experiences. Keep an open mind and watch a minimum of 4 episodes before you decide to drop or continue watching.

Durarara!!

An extremely entertaining, funny, and dynamic show that consists of gang wars, sociopathic informants, inhumanely strong bartenders, and Russian sushi.

Some honorable mentions

Attack on Titan

This anime has made it into mainstream media, so there has to be something going for it. People live behind walls, unable to leave in fear of giant humanoid beasts (the titans) that eat humans. The show follows a few characters in the corps that fights these titans.

Boku Dake Ga Inai Machi (Erased)

A murder mystery with a bit of supernatural.

Noragami

Based on Japanese mythology but takes place in a modern Japan setting, the show follows a god trying to make a living.
